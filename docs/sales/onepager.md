## The problem

Dallas-area basketball chapters are losing the recruiting battle — not because officials aren't interested, but because the follow-through breaks down. Word-of-mouth gets someone to a clinic. A text thread or a spreadsheet tries to track what happens next. Half of new recruits never finish onboarding, and nobody knows who dropped off or why until the schedule is short.

---

## What CrewCore Recruit does

CrewCore Recruit is a structured recruiting and onboarding system built specifically for officiating chapters. It replaces the spreadsheet and the group text with a purpose-built funnel:

1. **Public lead capture** — chapter-branded signup page collects contact, background, and motivation at the moment of interest
2. **Onboarding workflow** — each recruit moves through a defined sequence of steps (background check, FWAA membership, assessment, evaluations, clinic) with status tracked automatically
3. **Recruiter Command Center** — chapter staff see every recruit's current step, time-in-step, and completion status in one view
4. **Recruit status page** — recruits get a personal magic-link page showing exactly where they stand and what's next
5. **AI-ready** (Slice 4) — lead scoring, drop-off prediction, campaign drafting, shortage-zone targeting

**What it does not do:** replace Arbiter/AssignIt for game assignment. CrewCore Recruit owns the top of the funnel; chapters keep their existing assignment platform.

---

## Why now

DBOA is the founding chapter. As the first chapter on CrewCore, DBOA shapes the product — what workflow steps are standard, what the recruit experience feels like, what reporting the Command Center surfaces. The platform is already built and live.

## Pricing

### DBOA founding rate

| Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Base fee | **$4 / official / year** (standard rate: $5) |
| Success fee | **+ 10% of first-year dues** — new and transfer officials only, in the calendar year they join |
| Cutoff | December 31 roster snapshot — officials joining after Dec 31 roll to next year |

### What the base fee covers

The $4 per-official base is a whole-roster, year-round platform fee. CrewCore walks every official — new and veteran — through onboarding and clearance each season. This is not a per-recruit charge; it is the annual fee for the system that manages the full membership.

### DBOA dues (2026–27, nonrefundable)

| Member type | Dues |
|---|---|
| New | $125 |
| Returning (effective April 1) | $175 |
| Returning-from-inactive | $175 |
| Transfer | $175 |

### DBOA cost estimate

| Scenario | Calculation | Total |
|---|---|---|
| Base (steady state) | ~300 officials × $4 | $1,200 / year |
| Success fee (example) | 60 new officials × $125 × 10% | $750 |
| Heavy recruiting year | Base + success fee | ~$1,950 / year |
| Lean year (few new recruits) | Base only | ~$1,200 / year |

### Billing mechanics

Annual invoice, issued off the December 31 roster snapshot. The 10% success fee is calculated against the dues first-year officials actually paid during that calendar year. In year two, those officials convert to the $4 base count.

### Enforcement path

The 10% is invoice-based in year one. Once Stripe dues integration ships (Slice 3), the success fee becomes automatically attributable and collectible at the point of payment.

## What CrewCore is not

- Not a managed recruiting service (CrowdIQ ad campaigns are a separate service, not included)
- Not a game assignment platform
- Not a chapter takeover — DBOA keeps its leadership, bylaws, training culture, evaluation process, and school relationships
